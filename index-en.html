Our Story

More than a garden

In the heart of the city lies our healing plant garden - a place where ancient knowledge meets living soil. Since 1987 we have tended over 400 plant species here, from wild herbs to rare medicinal plants from around the world.

We believe every plant has a story to tell. Our gardeners know these stories - and love to share them.

The garden is not a museum. It grows, blooms, fades and grows again. Come as often as you like - it looks different every time.

Folk Medicine & Effects

What plants can do

No hocus-pocus - generational knowledge that science is increasingly confirming.

🌿

Ribwort Plantain

Plantago lanceolata

Aucubin and acteoside make it a natural expectorant. Placing leaves directly on insect bites actually works.

Application Cough syrup from fresh leaves · Compress for insect bites
🌼

Chamomile

Matricaria chamomilla

Alpha-bisabolol and chamazulene are anti-inflammatory - internally and externally.

Application Tea · Steam bath · Ointment for wounds and eczema
🌙

Valerian

Valeriana officinalis

Valerenic acid influences GABA receptors - gentler than sleeping pills, no dependency potential.

Application Tincture · Tea · Extract capsules for sleep disorders
🟠

Pot Marigold

Calendula officinalis

Flavonoids and saponins promote wound healing. A calendula ointment belongs in every medicine cabinet.

Application Ointment · Oil infusion · Tea for mucous membrane inflammation
💜

Lavender

Lavandula angustifolia

Linalool has a demonstrably calming effect on the nervous system - as a scent and internally.

Application Essential oil · Tea · Pillow scent · Tincture
🫚

St John's Wort

Hypericum perforatum

Hypericin and hyperforin show antidepressant effects. Watch for interactions - always consult a professional.

Application Red oil for muscles · Tea · Extract for mild depression
